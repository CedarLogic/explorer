Definition
A set of categories `{ cat_1, cat_2, ... cat_n }` of the field 2 are "solidary" if there exist some observations `(x,cat_1)`, some observations `(x,cat_2)`, ...some observations `(x,cat_n)` with a common category x of the field 1.

Each line of the graph represents a set. The red squares on the left are the solidary categories of the field 2 . The blue squares on the right are the categories of the field 1 which allow the solidarity.

The sets (lines) are sorted by decreasing cardinality.
Parameters
In the top slot, the categorical field 1 allowing the solidarity.
In the left slot, the categorical field 2 whose categories are solidary.
